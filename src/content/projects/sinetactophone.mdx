The Sinetactophone is a collaborative sonic/tactile instrument that explores expressive gesture through both air-gesture (theremin-adjacent distance sensing) and hands-on interaction.

Built initially as part of ITP Physical Computing, the project focused on interaction design (including a two-player interaction model) and sound design using an Arduino-driven sensor system mapped into Ableton.

Over time, the work expanded into iterative prototyping and play-testing, exploring enclosure geometry, sensor placement, and feedback elements (LEDs/visuals) to make the instrument feel more playable and legible to both performers and observers.

This project sits in a broader thread of "playable" interfaces: pairing discrete tactile controls with continuous spatial nuance so musical phrasing can live in the overlap.
